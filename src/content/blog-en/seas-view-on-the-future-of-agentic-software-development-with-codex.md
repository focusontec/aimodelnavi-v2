## Redefining the Development Process: The Paradigm Shift Codex Enables

Sea Limited, the Asian digital consumer platform giant, is fundamentally changing how software is developed by rolling out OpenAI’s Codex across its engineering teams.

David Chen, Sea’s Chief Product Officer, emphasized that this adoption is not merely about “coding efficiency,” but rather a transition toward **“AI-native software development.”** Instead of integrating AI as an auxiliary tool into traditional development workflows, this initiative aims to establish a new paradigm where AI leads the process from code generation to optimization.

## Why Codex, and Why Now?

The biggest bottleneck in engineering organizations often lies in balancing implementation speed with code consistency. By adopting Codex, Sea aims to accelerate progress in three key areas:

1. **Accelerated Development Cycles:** Delegating routine code writing to AI allows engineers to focus on higher-level architecture design and business logic.
2. **An AI-Native Approach:** Integrating AI from the earliest stages of development enables software designs optimized for how AI processes information.
3. **Rapid Deployment to Asian Markets:** In Asia’s fast-evolving digital landscape, drastically shortening the time-to-feature directly translates into a competitive edge.

## Toward an Agentic Development Environment

What’s particularly noteworthy is the vision for “agentic” (autonomous) development that goes beyond simple code autocompletion. With powerful models like Codex as a foundation, we are beginning to see a future where AI can autonomously support the entire workflow—from requirements gathering to implementation, testing, and deployment.

When a large-scale engineering organization like Sea Limited fully embraces Codex, it sets a benchmark for enterprise AI adoption moving forward. We are standing at a tipping point where AI evolves from being a “tool” to becoming a “co-developer.”
