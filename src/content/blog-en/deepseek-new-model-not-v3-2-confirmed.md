DeepSeek has made a subtle update to its official website, introducing what appears to be a new model. While the exact version name isn't explicitly stated, testing reveals it is highly unlikely to be the previously rumored "DeepSeek-V3.2," but rather an entirely new model with distinct characteristics.

Verified by DataLearnerAI, this new model demonstrates clear behavioral differences compared to earlier versions.

### Context Window and Knowledge Cutoff Evolution

The most striking discovery comes from the model's own responses: it supports up to **1 million tokens for input** and claims a **knowledge cutoff date of May 2025**. Previously, DeepSeek had ceased officially disclosing knowledge cutoff dates since the V3.1 release, with the DeepSeek-V3-0324 version citing a cutoff of July 2024.

The emergence of such a recent cutoff strongly hints at a major internal overhaul or a shift toward a next-generation model, potentially DeepSeek-V4. This leap in context window capacity and data recency provides significant advantages for AI agent applications and complex long-document analysis. Although an official announcement from DeepSeek is still pending, users can already access and benefit from these enhancements via the official site.
