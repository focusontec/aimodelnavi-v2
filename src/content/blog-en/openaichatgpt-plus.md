## National-Level AI Adoption: OpenAI's Partnership with the Republic of Malta

OpenAI has announced a partnership with the Republic of Malta to provide all its citizens with access to ChatGPT Plus. This is an extremely ambitious initiative, as it involves adopting AI tools at a national level rather than having individual users subscribe to plans, creating an environment where the entire population can utilize the latest AI models.

According to OpenAI's official announcement, the goal of this partnership is to democratize AI technology, enabling each citizen to master AI usage, thereby boosting individual productivity and enhancing the nation's overall competitiveness.

## Beyond Tool Provision: Designing for 'Reskilling'

A notable aspect of this initiative is not merely distributing a paid plan for free, but integrating AI as a social foundation and designing "reskilling (relearning)" for the entire population.

With the provision of ChatGPT Plus, citizens will be able to utilize advanced features such as:
- Access to the latest models with enhanced reasoning capabilities
- Use of multimodal functions like data analysis, image generation, and web browsing
- Creation and use of specialized AI tools like custom GPTs for specific applications

By having the entire population use these features on a daily basis, the aim is to naturally foster collaborative skills with AI, nurturing talent that can adapt flexibly to changes in the job market.

## Implications for Japanese Local Governments: A Benchmark for AI Adoption

Currently, in Japan, many local governments and government agencies are considering AI adoption, but often, this remains limited to an internal perspective of "improving staff work efficiency." However, Malta's case has an outward-looking perspective of "direct provision to citizens," offering insights for Japan's AI strategy in the following three points:

**1. Bridging the Digital Divide (AI Divide)**
By removing the barrier of paid plans, an environment is being built where anyone can use cutting-edge AI regardless of economic status. This ensures equal opportunities for skill acquisition.

**2. Elevating Practical Skills**
Instead of classroom-based training, by providing a "learning by using the tool" environment at the national level, it promotes highly effective reskilling.

**3. Rapid Implementation through Partnerships**
Rather than building systems in-house, by directly partnering with a global AI provider like OpenAI, it achieves a sense of speed in deploying the latest technology to citizens.

## Conclusion

The provision of ChatGPT Plus to all citizens by the Republic of Malta is a groundbreaking case that positions AI not merely as a convenient tool, but as "public infrastructure" for education and economic development. This approach, which identifies the source of national competitiveness in the AI era as "each citizen's ability to utilize AI" and guarantees it as a system, will have a significant impact on the future of digital government.
